Section 03 · Hardware

Display, Camera & Battery

Display

All three have top-tier 120Hz LTPO OLED panels.

  • Samsung: 3120 x 1440 — slight resolution edge, calibration and sharpness widely praised
  • Apple: 2868 x 1320, ~3000 nits outdoor brightness
  • Huawei: 2832 x 1280 (Pro), 3000 nits (Pro)

Daily use: all three are indistinguishable.

Camera System

  • Samsung: 200MP main (f/1.4) + 50MP ultrawide + 50MP 5x periscope + 10MP 3x tele — most complete focal length coverage
  • Apple: Triple 48MP (main f/1.6, ultrawide f/2.2, 5x tele f/2.8) — uniform across all three lenses, remains the video benchmark
  • Huawei: 50MP RYYB main (f/1.4–f/4.0 variable) + 40MP ultrawide + 48MP macro tele (4x) — XMAGE algorithm delivers distinctive color style and strong low-light performance

Battery & Charging

  • Huawei: 5750 mAh (Pro) / 6000 mAh (Pro Max) + 100W SuperCharge — clear leader, far superior
  • Samsung: 5000 mAh, 45W wired
  • Apple: ~4832 mAh, ~40W (50% in 20 min), 25W MagSafe

Note: iPhone power efficiency under light loads can sometimes result in comparable or better real-world battery life than some larger-battery Android phones. All three are IP68 rated.

Source: II. Hardware Configuration & Hardware Analysis
Hardware breakdown: all three displays are excellent 120Hz LTPO OLED panels. Samsung has a slight resolution advantage but daily use is indistinguishable. For cameras, Samsung offers the most complete focal length coverage with 200MP main plus dual telephoto. Apple achieves uniform 48MP across all three lenses and remains the video benchmark. Huawei continues with variable aperture (f/1.4-f/4.0) plus RYYB sensor delivering distinctive color. Battery: Huawei leads by a large margin with 5750-6000 mAh and 100W charging. Apple at ~4832 mAh / 40W is the weakest on paper but its power efficiency can close the gap in real-world use.

Section 04 · User Experience

Gaming, OS & Signal Quality

Gaming Performance

  • Apple: 1h Genshin Impact max settings: ≥59 fps, max temp ~41°C — most reliable frame stability, best-ever iPhone thermals with first VC vapor chamber
  • Huawei Pro Max: Wind Speed edition uses active cooling — excels in sustained high-load gaming; standard model: stable 59 fps Genshin max settings, good long-session thermals
  • Samsung: High-load mobile games avg 54 fps, some dips — highest burst performance but throttles after ~4 minutes

OS & Smoothness

  • iOS 26: Very high smoothness, though some users report occasional iOS 26 frame drops. Emphasizes simplicity and fluidity.
  • One UI 8.5 (Android 16): Animations smooth, no stutter. Most feature-rich and customisable. 7 years of OS & security updates — longest among flagships.
  • HarmonyOS 6.0 (Pure Harmony): Daily smoothness greatly improved, heavy-load frame stability +50%. Notably, keeps apps launching and switching smoothly even when storage is >80% full.

Signal & Thermals

  • Huawei: Lingxi antenna technology — traditional strength, most reliable choice for signal
  • Apple: First VC vapor chamber + unibody aluminum shell, warm but not hot. Signal reputation is middling.
  • Samsung: Graphene cooling area +25%, notable improvement over previous gen. Weak-network stability needs refinement.
Source: III. System & User Experience Summary
User experience breakdown: Gamers should note that Apple offers the most reliable frame stability and best-ever iPhone thermals. Huawei's Pro Max Wind Speed edition uses active cooling to excel in sustained high-load gaming. Samsung has the highest burst performance but throttles after about 4 minutes. For daily use, all three are extremely smooth. Huawei notably keeps apps launching smoothly even when storage exceeds 80% full. Signal: Huawei, drawing on its telecom heritage and Lingxi antenna technology, remains the most reliable choice.

  • Unique hardware: Apple: 3D structured-light Face ID, titanium/aluminum build. Samsung: hardware-level privacy screen, S Pen (Ultra). Huawei: Kunlun Glass, variable-aperture main camera, active cooling (Pro Max Wind Speed).

Section 06 · Recommendations

Which Phone Is Right for You?

Choose iPhone 17 Pro Max if you:

  • Are deeply invested in the Apple ecosystem (Mac, iPad, AirPods) and want seamless integration
  • Prioritise industry-leading video recording and post-production (content creator / vlogger)
  • Seek extreme long-term smoothness and high resale value
  • Demand maximum privacy for AI (Apple Intelligence runs fully on-device)
  • Don't mind 40W charging or the lack of satellite calling

Choose Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra if you:

  • Are a heavy mobile gamer who values GPU burst power (accepting throttling after a few minutes)
  • Want the absolute best display — Samsung's calibration and sharpness are widely praised
  • Need the S Pen for note-taking, drawing, or office work
  • Prefer Samsung/Windows ecosystem synergy and value 7 years of OS updates
  • Don't care about charging speed (45W) or 5000mAh battery endurance

Choose Huawei Mate 80 Pro / Pro Max if you:

  • Have a genuine need for satellite communication (outdoor workers, adventurers) — get the Pro Max Wind Speed edition
  • Are a business user who values excellent signal quality and HarmonyOS synergy with domestic office devices
  • Demand long battery life and fast charging (5750mAh + 100W is among the best in flagships)
  • Have a tighter budget but still want a flagship experience (current price advantage is huge)
  • Prefer pure HarmonyOS and want a locally-optimised software and AI experience
Source: VI. Comprehensive Buying Advice
Three distinct user profiles emerge. Apple for ecosystem users and content creators who prioritize video. Samsung for spec chasers, gamers, and S Pen users. Huawei for satellite needs, business users, battery life, and unmatched value at around ¥4700. The right phone depends entirely on what you prioritize most. There is no single "best" — only the best for you.
